Proteins were ranked based on Molar Fraction (Mol %) for Muddy Brown Granular Cast Samples. Molar fraction was calculated using Exponentially Modified Protein Abundance Index (emPAI)1 equation 3. Fractional abundance was ranked for each protein within each sample. Mean ranks were calculated across all patient samples and displayed as “Mean Rank Across Individual MBGC Samples”. Global ranks were tabulated by ordering the mean ranks from lowest to highest. Sometimes proteins are not discovered in every sample, therefore, “Protein Found Out of 12 Samples” indicates the number of MBGC samples in which a given protein was discovered. Individual protein ranks for MBGCs from each patient are provided on the right-hand side. Low number rank = higher abundance. Ishihama Y, Oda Y, Tabata T, Sato T, Nagasu T, Rappsilber J, Mann M. Exponentially modified protein abundance index (emPAI) for estimation of absolute protein amount in proteomics by the number of sequenced peptides per protein. Mol Cell Proteomics. 2005 Sep;4(9):1265-72. doi: 10.1074/mcp.M500061-MCP200. PMID: 15958392.
